About the Artist

Suzanne Frie, currently based in Hawaii, is a primarily self-taught American artist that signs all work by her middle "old family" name of Rittenhouse. The artist is well versed in various mediums like water color, oil, and acrylic, but has refined her skill in the use of tissue paper to create a body of work that mimics such artists as John Singer Sargent, Claude Monet, John White Alexander and other impressionists.

The artist's practice is to avoid using paper that is cut but, rather, to rip the paper to achieve the impressionistic look as the rips mimic brush strokes. Works include landscapes, seascapes, and girls in white dresses with Chinese umbrellas. The artist continues to be enchanted with scenes and subjects of an era gone by and themes similar to the artists of the late 1800's.

In a time where schools’ available funds are being cut, thus eliminating orrestricting education in the fine arts, Suzanne donates her time at the local elementary school teaching afternoon art lessons to ,primarily, military children 2-6th grade in various mediums including painting, sculpture and artworks that use recyclable materials. Suzanne is passionate about charity and donating works of art to local and national organizations that promote conversation of the environment, local causes and the enrichment of education for youth.

Chevron Series

Chevron Paper Painting: Rittenhouse's picture of a woman covered with a semi-transparent chevron patterned blanket is partially cropped to emphasize the trunk region of the body, with an erotic focus of the breasts and pubic region. The hip bone is carved out with a strong contour line of dark blue and it trusting in an upward motion, reminiscent of the bold contour lines of Klimt and Schiele. Though not strong in the photograph, the red pubic hair rises above the chevron pattern in the image, in a tempting manner that leads to the fleshy inner thigh of the spread legs. The milky creaminess of the woman's skin is partially viewed through the blanket near the lower stomach and upper hip region giving a sense of movement as if the woman were turning in her sleep under a sheer blanket.

The Paper Painter uses bold contour lines of the female form to serve as a mechanism of exploration between chevron-patterned fabric and its relationship to the nude body. How the fabric and fold, drape and respond to the body in different positions. The entire image, including the chevron patterns are carefully constructed out of layered tissue paper.

All paintings are 14 ½" x 19"/ea

Keiki Collection

Keiki is the Hawaiian term used for baby or child. This series of tissue paper paintings celebrate Polynesian children with the focus of conveying the importance of keeping their culture and history alive. The blankets with various patterns are symbolic of the traditional Hawaiian quilts and have patterns that are still used to day to represent the culture. Some of the children have traditional head leis used within the culture like the Maile Lei.

All paintings are 48" x 29 ¾"
