The Art of Suzanne Rittenhouse

About the Artist

Suzanne Frie, currently based in Hawaii, is a primarily self-taught American artist that signs all work by her middle "old family" name of Rittenhouse. The artist is well versed in various mediums like water color, oil, and acylic, but has refined her skill in the use of tissue paper to create a body of work that mimics such artists as John Signer Sargent, Claude Monet, John While Alexander and other impressionists.

The artist's practice is to avoid using paper that is cut but, rather, to rip the paper to achieve the impressionistic look as the rips mimic brush strokes. Works include landscapes, seascapes, girls in white dresses and Chinese umbrellas. The artist continues to be enchanted with scenes and subjects of an era gone by and themes similar to the artist's of the late 1800's.

Keiki Collection

Keiki is the Hawaiian term used for baby or child. This series of tissue paper paintings celebrate Polynesian children with the focus of conveying the importance of keeping their culture and history alive. The blankets with various patterns are symbolic of the traditional Hawaiian quilts and have patterns that are still used to day to represent the culture. Some of the children have traditional head leis used within the culture like the Maile Lei. 48" x 29 ¾"
