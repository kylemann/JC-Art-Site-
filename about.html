Suzanne Frie

Artist

A primarily self-taught American artist that signs all work by her middle "old family" name of Rittenhouse. The artist is well versed in various mediums like water color, oil, and acylic, but has refined her skill in the use of tissue paper to create a body of work that mimics such artists as John Signer Sargent, Claude Monet, John While Alexander and other impressionists. Suzanne Rittenhouse Frie is progressive in her large paper works, dispensing with the traditional academic approaches learned under masters and at University. She does on engage with careful under-paintings or drawings but prefers to work from watercolor sketches or computer manipulated sketches that are rendered in such a ways as to encourage impressionism. She works directly on the canvas of paper that she creates herself with her loaded palette of papers in over 72 different colors. This approach is very similar to impressionism in approach as she can only use solid pure color to render the image and relies on proper placement of tones of colored tissue paper. The approach permits spontaneous colors that are not bound to tedious under-drawings or sketches and is a markedly different approach from traditional fine art methods.

Works can take anywhere from several days to months depending upon the size of the work and the attention to detail and the amount of impressionism as all works are comprised of rips of tissue paper that have been layered upon each other to develop the color aspect of the art work. The artist's practice is to avoid using paper that is cut but, rather, to rip the paper to achieve the impressionistic look as the rips mimic brush strokes. Works include landscapes and seascapes as small as 14 x 19 inches to large works of girls in white dress and Chinese umbrellas measuring sizes of 42 x 72 inches. The artist continues to be enchanted with scenes and subjects of an era gone by and themes similar to the artist's of the late 1800's.
